# Glossary

<div class="landing-hero">
  <p>Core MS terms for quick reference while reading topic pages and paper cards.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-grid">
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Autoimmune disease</strong><span>A condition where the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Axon</strong><span>The long, thin projection of a nerve cell that conducts electrical signals.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>B cell</strong><span>A type of white blood cell that produces antibodies; increasingly recognized as important in MS.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Blood-brain barrier (BBB)</strong><span>A selective barrier between blood vessels and the brain that normally prevents immune cells from entering the CNS.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Central nervous system (CNS)</strong><span>The brain and spinal cord.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)</strong><span>The fluid that surrounds the brain and spinal cord; analyzed to help diagnose MS.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Clinically isolated syndrome (CIS)</strong><span>A first episode of neurological symptoms suggestive of MS.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Cytokine</strong><span>A small signaling protein released by cells that affects the behavior of other cells; key players in inflammation.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Demyelination</strong><span>Loss or damage to myelin, the insulating sheath around nerve fibers.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Disease-modifying therapy (DMT)</strong><span>A drug that reduces the frequency and severity of MS attacks and slows disability progression.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>EDSS</strong><span>Expanded Disability Status Scale; the most common measure of disability in MS clinical trials.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Epstein-Barr virus (EBV)</strong><span>A common herpesvirus strongly linked to MS risk; nearly all MS patients have been infected with EBV.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis (EAE)</strong><span>An animal model of MS where immune attacks on myelin are induced in mice or rats.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Gadolinium</strong><span>A contrast agent used in MRI to highlight active inflammation (gadolinium-enhancing lesions indicate recent immune activity).</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>GWAS</strong><span>Genome-wide association study; a method to find genetic variants associated with disease risk.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>HLA-DRB1</strong><span>A gene in the human leukocyte antigen system; HLA-DRB1*15:01 is the strongest known genetic MS risk variant.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Lesion</strong><span>An area of tissue damage; in MS, lesions are areas of demyelination visible on MRI.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Microglia</strong><span>Immune cells resident in the brain; they can drive inflammation or promote repair.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Myelin</strong><span>The fatty insulating sheath around nerve fibers that enables fast signal conduction.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Neurofilament light chain (NfL)</strong><span>A protein released from damaged nerve fibers; used as a blood biomarker of neuronal injury in MS.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Oligoclonal bands (OCBs)</strong><span>Antibodies found in CSF but not blood, indicating immune activity within the CNS; present in most MS cases.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Oligodendrocyte</strong><span>The cell type that produces myelin in the CNS.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Oligodendrocyte precursor cell (OPC)</strong><span>A stem-like cell that can mature into a myelin-producing oligodendrocyte.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Primary progressive MS (PPMS)</strong><span>A form of MS with steady worsening from onset, without distinct relapses.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Relapsing-remitting MS (RRMS)</strong><span>The most common form of MS, with episodic relapses followed by partial or full recovery.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Remyelination</strong><span>Repair of damaged myelin; a key therapeutic goal in MS research.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>Secondary progressive MS (SPMS)</strong><span>A phase that can follow RRMS and is marked by gradual worsening independent of relapses.</span></div>
  <div class="glossary-item"><strong>T cell</strong><span>A white blood cell type that attacks targets or regulates immune responses; both CD4+ and CD8+ cells are relevant in MS.</span></div>
</div>
