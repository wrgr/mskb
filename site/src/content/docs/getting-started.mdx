## What every concept page shows you

- **Difficulty**: 1 (introductory) to 5 (specialist).
- **Learning objectives**: Tagged with Bloom's level — Remember, Understand, Apply, Analyze. What you should be able to do after working through the concept.
- **Prerequisites**: Concepts you should understand first.
- **Specializes into / related**: Where to go next.
- **Key questions**: Self-check questions to test your understanding.
- **Papers**: 3–7 foundational references from the citation graph.
- **Resources**: Videos, tools, and datasets curated with a quality rubric (accuracy, clarity, utility, accessibility).

## Topic pages vs. concept pages

- **Concept** pages live under [`/concepts/`](/mskb/concepts/). They're written for learners, one concept at a time, with prerequisites and curated resources.
- **Topic** pages live under [`/topics/`](/mskb/topics/). They're *derived from the citation graph* — each topic is a cluster of papers that cite each other heavily. Topic pages are longer and denser, useful when you want to scan the full reading list for a research cluster.

If you are new to MS, start on a concept or a pathway. Use topic pages as reference.

## Language levels on paper cards

- **1–2** — plain wording in the plain-English summary.
- **3** — moderate technical vocabulary.
- **4–5** — specialist terminology density.

Filter by language level in the explorer or use the difficulty field on the concept hub.

## Corpus gaps & audit notes

MSKB is an in-progress map of the MS literature, not a canonical index. We track known gaps publicly.

<Aside type="caution" title="Known seed-level gaps">
The current corpus is biased toward recent work (2018+) because expansion seeds dominate the current intake. Known under-represented sources include:

- **UCSF MS Center** — Stephen Hauser, Bruce Cree, Jorge Oksenberg, Roland Martin's group. Foundational B-cell and anti-CD20 work, genetic risk studies.
- **Johns Hopkins** — Peter Calabresi, **Ellen Mowry**. Vitamin D, PIRA, pediatric MS, real-world outcomes.
- **Pre-2010 landmarks** generally — Lassmann (pathology), Trapp (axonal damage), McDonald criteria lineage.

These are being addressed via the landmark seed curation process documented in [`EDUCATOR_INTEGRATION_GUIDE.md`](https://github.com/wrgr/mskb/blob/main/EDUCATOR_INTEGRATION_GUIDE.md) and [`SEED_INTAKE_GOVERNANCE.md`](https://github.com/wrgr/mskb/blob/main/SEED_INTAKE_GOVERNANCE.md). If you spot another gap, open an issue or PR on [wrgr/mskb](https://github.com/wrgr/mskb).
</Aside>

## Research spectrum

- **Pathogenesis & Immunology** — immune mechanisms, myelin biology, EBV, remyelination.
- **Imaging & Biomarkers** — MRI protocols, OCT, lesion heterogeneity, NfL / GFAP markers.
- **Clinical Trials & Therapeutics** — DMT studies, efficacy / safety comparisons, emerging therapies.
- **Clinical Care & Management** — diagnosis criteria, phenotypes, care pathways.
- **Epidemiology & Population Health** — GWAS, environmental risk, prevalence, disparities, pediatric MS.
