Multiple sclerosis is a chronic, immune-mediated disease of the central nervous system in which the immune system attacks myelin — the insulating sheath around nerve fibres — leading to inflammation, demyelination, and eventually axonal injury and neurodegeneration. MS is clinically diverse: some patients experience episodic relapses followed by recovery, others accumulate disability steadily.

## Key questions to ask yourself

- What makes MS different from other neurological diseases?
- Why does MS affect the brain and spinal cord specifically, and not the peripheral nervous system?
- What clinical and imaging features move a patient from "possible" to "definite" MS?
