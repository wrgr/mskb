You cannot read the MS literature productively without a working mental model of adaptive immunity. This concept sets the floor: what T cells and B cells do, how antigen presentation works (HLA), and how tolerance normally prevents the immune system from attacking self.

## Learning objectives

<ul class="concept-objectives">
  <li>Describe the basic roles of T cells, B cells, and antigen-presenting cells.</li>
  <li>Explain central and peripheral tolerance and how they can fail.</li>
  <li>Use citation-backed readings to connect immune-system fundamentals to downstream MS mechanisms and therapies.</li>
</ul>

## Key questions to ask yourself

- Which core immune pathways are required to understand most MS mechanism papers?
- Where does normal immune tolerance fail in MS, and what evidence supports each failure mode?
- Which foundational immunology findings are directly actionable for current MS therapeutics?
