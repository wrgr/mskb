## Learning objectives

- Apply the core McDonald framework: dissemination in space (DIS) and in time (DIT)
- Describe how the 2017 and 2024 revisions changed the criteria (CSF oligoclonal bands, kappa free light chains, central vein sign, paramagnetic rim lesions)
- Explain why early diagnosis matters for treatment initiation
- Recognise when to suspect a differential diagnosis (NMO-SD, MOG-AD)

## Core idea

McDonald criteria formalise the requirement for CNS lesions disseminated in space (≥2 distinct regions) and time (evidence of ongoing lesion activity). The 2024 revision adds the central vein sign (CVS) and paramagnetic rim lesions (PRLs) as positive diagnostic markers, and kappa free light chains in CSF as an alternative to oligoclonal bands. These changes aim to increase diagnostic specificity, especially in atypical presentations.
