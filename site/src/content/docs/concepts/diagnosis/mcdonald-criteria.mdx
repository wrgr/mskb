The McDonald criteria formalize the diagnosis of MS by requiring objective evidence of CNS lesions **disseminated in space (DIS)** and **disseminated in time (DIT)**, with alternative diagnoses excluded. In practice, this means combining a clinically compatible presentation with MRI — and increasingly, CSF oligoclonal bands can substitute for DIT.

## Learning objectives

<ul class="concept-objectives">
  <li>Apply the current McDonald criteria to a clinical case with imaging.</li>
  <li>Interpret the standard MRI sequences used for MS — T2/FLAIR, T1 with gadolinium, 3D volumetric acquisitions.</li>
  <li>Recognize paramagnetic rim lesions and slowly expanding lesions as emerging biomarkers of chronic smoldering disease.</li>
</ul>

## Key questions

- What does "dissemination in space and time" actually mean, and how is each shown on MRI?
- When can CSF oligoclonal bands substitute for dissemination in time?
- How do you distinguish MS lesions from NMO, MOG-AD, or small-vessel disease lesions on MRI?
