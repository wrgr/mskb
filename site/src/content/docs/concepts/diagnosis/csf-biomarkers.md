## Learning objectives

- Explain how CSF oligoclonal bands (OCBs) support an MS diagnosis and substitute for DIT
- Describe kappa free light chains as an alternative to OCBs in the 2024 criteria
- Interpret serum neurofilament light chain (NfL) as a marker of neuroaxonal injury
- Distinguish diagnostic biomarkers (OCBs, NfL at diagnosis) from monitoring biomarkers (NfL for treatment response)

- [McDonald Criteria](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/mcdonald-criteria/)

## Key questions

- What is the sensitivity and specificity of OCBs for MS?
- Why might NfL be elevated in a patient with no new MRI lesions?
- How do blood NfL levels compare to CSF NfL?

## Core idea

CSF oligoclonal IgG bands (OCBs), present in ~85-95% of MS patients, reflect intrathecal immunoglobulin synthesis and provide DIT evidence even after a single attack. The 2024 McDonald criteria now accept kappa free light chains (elevated in CSF vs serum) as an equivalent. Serum NfL is a sensitive but non-specific marker of axonal injury that correlates with relapse rate, brain volume loss, and disability progression; serial measurements inform treatment decisions. GFAP reflects astrocytic injury and is elevated in progressive MS.
