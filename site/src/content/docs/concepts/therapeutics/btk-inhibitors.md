## Core idea

BTK (Bruton's tyrosine kinase) is expressed in B cells, plasma cells, and myeloid cells including microglia. CNS-penetrant BTK inhibitors (tolebrutinib, fenebrutinib, evobrutinib) aim to suppress both peripheral B-cell activation and microglial chronic active lesion activity — the proposed driver of smoldering/progressive MS. Tolebrutinib (HERCULES trial, 2023) met its primary endpoint in non-relapsing SPMS, reducing CDP, but a hepatotoxicity signal (rare liver injury) led to clinical holds and label warnings. First approvals expected 2025-2026.
