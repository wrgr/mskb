## Learning objectives

- Summarise the evidence that EBV is a near-necessary cause of MS
- Explain the mechanistic hypotheses linking EBV to autoimmunity (molecular mimicry, bystander activation)
- Describe the vitamin D latitude hypothesis and its limitations
- List modifiable environmental factors with the strongest evidence (smoking, obesity in adolescence)

- [Genetic Susceptibility in MS](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/genetic-susceptibility/)

## Key questions

- Does the Bjornevik (2022) military cohort study prove EBV causes MS?
- Why does smoking approximately double MS risk?
- Could a preventive EBV vaccine reduce MS incidence?

## Core idea

The 2022 Bjornevik et al. (*Science*) military cohort study found that EBV infection preceded MS diagnosis in >99% of cases, with a 32-fold increased risk post-EBV. Proposed mechanisms include molecular mimicry (GlialCAM/EBNA1 cross-reactivity), persistent EBV-infected B cells driving autoimmunity, and bystander activation. Vitamin D deficiency correlates with MS risk but interventional trials have been inconclusive. Smoking confers ~1.5–2× risk independently.
