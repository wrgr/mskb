## Learning objectives

- State the contribution of HLA-DRB1*15:01 to MS risk
- Describe findings from the largest MS GWAS (>200 non-HLA loci)
- Explain gene-environment interaction with EBV and vitamin D
- Distinguish heritability from determinism in a complex disease

- [Immune System Basics for MS](/mskb/concepts/foundations/immune-system-basics/)

## Key questions

- Why does MS run in families yet behave like a complex polygenic trait?
- What does HLA-DRB1*15:01 actually do to increase risk?
- How does EBV interact with HLA genotype?

## Core idea

The strongest genetic risk factor for MS is HLA-DRB1*15:01 (OR ~3), which encodes an MHC class II molecule with high affinity for myelin peptides. The 2023 IMSGC GWAS (>40 000 cases) identified 236 independent susceptibility variants, most in immune-related genes (IL-2RA, IL-7R, TNFRSF1A, CLEC16A). Overall heritability is ~50%, with the remaining risk attributable to environment — particularly Epstein-Barr virus infection, which is now considered a near-necessary precondition for MS.
