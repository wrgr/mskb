## Core idea

The strongest genetic risk factor for MS is HLA-DRB1*15:01 (OR ~3), which encodes an MHC class II molecule with high affinity for myelin peptides. The 2023 IMSGC GWAS (>40 000 cases) identified 236 independent susceptibility variants, most in immune-related genes (IL-2RA, IL-7R, TNFRSF1A, CLEC16A). Overall heritability is ~50%, with the remaining risk attributable to environment — particularly Epstein-Barr virus infection, which is now considered a near-necessary precondition for MS.
