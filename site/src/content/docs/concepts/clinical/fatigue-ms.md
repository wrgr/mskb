## Key questions

- Why is fatigue underreported and undertreated despite being the #1 patient complaint?
- What is the difference between lassitude (MS-specific fatigue) and general tiredness?
- Is fatigue a symptom of inflammation, neurodegeneration, or both?

## Core idea

Fatigue affects >80% of people with MS (pwMS) and is the symptom most strongly associated with reduced employment, social participation, and quality of life. Two mechanistic subtypes are recognised: primary fatigue (directly caused by CNS pathology — thalamic involvement, inflammatory cytokines, disrupted cortical connectivity) and secondary fatigue (from insomnia, depression, pain, medication side effects). The MFIS (Modified Fatigue Impact Scale) and FSS (Fatigue Severity Scale) are validated instruments; the SDMT correlates with fatigue independently. Exercise has the strongest RCT evidence; cooling strategies, CBT, and energy conservation are also effective. Modafinil and amantadine have modest RCT evidence.

## Patient perspective

Fatigue is consistently rated as the most burdensome MS symptom by patients — yet surveys show it is frequently not addressed in clinical consultations. The invisibility of fatigue contributes to employment discrimination and social isolation. Patient advocacy organisations (MSIF, NMSS) highlight fatigue literacy as a priority for both patients and clinicians.
