## Learning objectives

- Describe how MS disease activity typically changes during pregnancy and postpartum
- Classify DMTs by pregnancy safety (Category A–D equivalent / teratogenic risk)
- Counsel a patient on DMT washout timing and bridging strategies before conception
- Identify which DMTs have human safety data and which require discontinuation pre-conception

## Prerequisites

- [DMT Overview](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/dmt-overview/)

## Key questions

- Why does disease activity decline in the third trimester?
- What is the evidence for postpartum relapse risk and how should it be managed?
- Which DMTs are contraindicated in pregnancy and for how long before conception?

## Core idea

Pregnancy exerts an immunomodulatory effect — Th1 immunity is suppressed in the third trimester, reducing relapse rates by ~70%. This is followed by a postpartum rebound (increased relapse risk in the first 3 months). Interferon-beta and glatiramer acetate have the most reassuring safety data. Natalizumab can be continued until 30 weeks with careful benefit-risk assessment. Cladribine, alemtuzumab, fingolimod, and teriflunomide carry significant teratogenic risk requiring adequate washout. Planning around the postpartum period with early DMT reinstatement is standard practice.
