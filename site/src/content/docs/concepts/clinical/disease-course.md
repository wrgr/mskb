## Learning objectives

- Describe the typical disease course from CIS through RRMS to SPMS using natural history data
- Interpret EDSS scores and their limitations as a disability measure
- Distinguish relapse-associated worsening from progression independent of relapse activity (PIRA)
- Summarise predictors of long-term disability (lesion burden, brain atrophy, spinal cord involvement)

## Prerequisites

- [MS Phenotypes](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/ms-phenotypes/)

## Key questions

- What fraction of untreated RRMS patients reach EDSS 6 (walking aid required) by 20 years?
- How does the EDSS capture — and fail to capture — MS-related disability?
- What does "smoldering MS" add to the relapsing/progressive dichotomy?

## Core idea

Longitudinal cohort studies (London Ontario, Gothenburg) established that without treatment, ~50% of RRMS patients reach EDSS 6 by 15 years, and ~90% transition to SPMS by 25 years. The rate of disability progression correlates with spinal cord atrophy, T1 black hole volume, and brain parenchymal fraction. Modern DMTs substantially reduce relapse rates and delay SPMS conversion, but do not fully arrest disability accumulation, which is increasingly attributable to PIRA — progression independent of relapse activity.
