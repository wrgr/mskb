This is the **Layer A** learner map for MSKB. Each concept is a standalone page with learning objectives, prerequisites, recommended papers from the citation graph, and curated non-paper resources (videos, tools, datasets).

The concept hierarchy is taken from [`LEARNING_CONCEPT_ONTOLOGY.md`](https://github.com/wrgr/mskb/blob/main/LEARNING_CONCEPT_ONTOLOGY.md) and is grounded in Bloom's taxonomy + cognitive load theory. Concept pages marked with detail are fully written; the rest are stubs seeded from the ontology and being filled in over time.

## Three learner pathways

If you're not sure which concept to open first, follow a prebuilt pathway:

- **[Clinical pathway](/mskb/pathways/clinical/)** — symptom → diagnosis → classification → treatment.
- **[Mechanistic pathway](/mskb/pathways/mechanistic/)** — immune basics → inflammation → demyelination → drug targets.
- **[Emerging topics](/mskb/pathways/emerging/)** — PIRA, smoldering MS, paramagnetic rim lesions, BTK inhibitors.

## How concept pages relate to topic pages

- A **concept** is a learner-centered unit. It has prerequisites, learning objectives, and curated resources.
- A **[citation topic](/mskb/topics/)** is a cluster of papers grouped by Louvain community detection on the citation graph.

A concept may pull papers from several citation topics, and a topic may contribute to several concepts. The two layers are complementary — concepts answer *"what should I learn?"*, topics answer *"what does the research landscape look like?"*.
