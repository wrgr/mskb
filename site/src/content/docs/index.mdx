## What every concept page gives you

Every concept page follows the same structure, so you always know where you are:

- **Learning objectives** — what you should be able to do, tagged by Bloom's level (Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze)
- **Prerequisites** — concepts to understand first, with direct links
- **Key questions** — self-check prompts to test your understanding before moving on
- **Papers** — 3–7 foundational references drawn from the citation graph
- **Resources** — videos, tools, and datasets vetted against a quality rubric

The citation graph, concept map, and resources are kept separate on purpose: each answers a different question. *What is the research landscape?* *What should I learn next?* *How do I learn it?*

## Built openly — open to review

MSKB is assembled from an OpenAlex-derived bibliometric pipeline (retrieve → score → cluster → distill), layered with a hand-curated concept ontology. The corpus begins with **40 hand-curated seed papers** across 18 MS research topics, expanded via citation networks and filtered by cross-seed connectivity, citation scores, and expert review.

Every design decision is documented. Every gap is tracked publicly. If you are a researcher, clinician, or advanced student who spots an error or a missing thread, reach out — expert eyes improve this resource for everyone.

<Aside type="note" title="AI-assembled, expert-reviewed — what that means">
MSKB is a **learning engineering experiment** built to support student access to MS research. The corpus was assembled and annotated with AI assistance (bibliometric pipeline + Claude API distillation). Plain-language summaries are AI-generated from the primary literature.

Verify critical clinical claims against primary sources and current guidelines before applying them. This resource is not a substitute for clinical judgment or primary literature review.

Errors, omissions, and sharper framings are genuinely welcome — they improve the resource for everyone who uses it.
