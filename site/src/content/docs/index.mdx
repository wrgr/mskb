## What makes a concept page

Every concept page has the same structure:

- **Learning objectives** — what you should be able to do after you understand it (tagged with Bloom's level)
- **Prerequisites** — concepts you should understand first
- **Specializes into / related** — where to go next
- **Papers** — 3–7 foundational references from the citation graph
- **Resources** — videos, tools, and datasets curated against a quality rubric

Concepts, citations, and resources are kept **separate** on purpose. The citation graph answers *"what's the research landscape?"*; the concept map answers *"what should I learn next?"*; resources answer *"how do I learn it?"*.

## How this was built

MSKB is built from an OpenAlex-derived bibliometric pipeline (retrieve → score → cluster → distill) layered with a hand-curated concept ontology grounded in Bloom's taxonomy and cognitive load theory. The corpus starts from **36 hand-curated seed papers** across 18 MS research topics, expanded via one-hop citation networks and filtered by cross-seed connectivity and within-subdomain citation scores. Every design decision is documented.
