This pathway moves from the first clinical encounter through diagnosis, phenotyping, treatment selection, and monitoring. It is designed for learners whose entry point is the patient — a student on a neurology rotation, a pre-med with a family member with MS, or a trainee who wants to understand treatment decisions before diving into mechanism.

<Aside type="tip">
Each step links to a concept page with learning objectives, prerequisites, and curated papers. Work through them in order, or jump in at the step where you already have the foundation.
</Aside>

## Pathway sequence

<Steps>

1. **[What is MS?](/mskb/concepts/foundations/what-is-ms/)**

   Start here. Define MS, distinguish it from NMO and MOGAD, and recognize the four phenotypes (RRMS, PPMS, SPMS, CIS). This is the vocabulary everything else builds on.

2. **[MS epidemiology & natural history](/mskb/concepts/foundations/epidemiology/)**

   Who gets MS, where, and what happens over time without treatment? The Lyon cohort data (Confavreux) and the Atlas of MS give the demographic and long-term picture.

3. **[Diagnostic criteria & MRI](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/mcdonald-criteria/)**

   How do we diagnose MS from a combination of clinical presentation, MRI findings, and CSF? Apply the McDonald 2024 criteria. Learn to read T2, T1-gadolinium, FLAIR, and spinal cord imaging.

4. **[MS phenotypes](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/phenotypes/)**

   CIS, RRMS, SPMS, PPMS — what distinguishes them clinically and biologically, and why the distinction drives treatment choice.

5. **[CSF biomarkers](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/biomarkers/)**

   Oligoclonal bands, IgG index, and the emerging blood-based markers (NfL, GFAP) that are reshaping how we monitor disease between clinic visits.

6. **[DMT overview](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/dmt-overview/)**

   The three generations of disease-modifying therapy. What each class does, what level of efficacy to expect, and the escalation-vs.-early-high-efficacy debate.

7. **[B cell-targeted therapies](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/b-cell-therapies/)**

   Why anti-CD20 agents (ocrelizumab, ofatumumab) became the dominant treatment for active RRMS and PPMS. Mechanism, pivotal trials (OPERA, ORATORIO), monitoring, and infection risk.

8. **[Treatment monitoring & switches](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/monitoring-switches/)**

   When is a treatment working? What triggers a switch? How do MRI, clinical relapses, and biomarkers inform treatment decisions?

9. **[Disease course & progression](/mskb/concepts/clinical/disease-course/)**

   Relapse vs. PIRA. How disability accumulates. EDSS and its limitations. What smoldering MS means for long-term outcomes.

10. **[Special populations](/mskb/concepts/clinical/special-populations/)**

    Pregnancy, pediatric MS, older adults, and sex differences in disease course and treatment tolerance.

</Steps>
