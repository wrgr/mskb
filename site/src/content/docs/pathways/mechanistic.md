This pathway is for learners whose starting question is **"why does MS happen?"** Work through it top to bottom.

## Steps

1. **[Immune-system basics for MS](/mskb/concepts/foundations/immune-system-basics/)** — minimum immunology.
2. **[T cell biology](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/t-cell-biology/)** and **[B cell biology](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/b-cell-biology/)**.
3. **[Antigen presentation and HLA](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/antigen-presentation-hla/)** — why HLA-DRB1\*15:01 is the strongest MS risk allele.
4. **[Autoimmunity mechanisms](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/autoimmunity-mechanisms/)** — how tolerance fails.
5. **[Genetic susceptibility](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/genetic-susceptibility/)** and **[environmental risk factors](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/environmental-risk-factors/)**.
6. **[Epstein–Barr virus and MS](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/ebv-and-ms/)** — the strongest environmental risk factor.
7. **[Molecular mimicry](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/molecular-mimicry/)** — EBNA1 → GlialCAM.
8. **[Neuroinflammation pathways](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/neuroinflammation/)** — cytokines, microglia, innate-adaptive crosstalk.
9. **[Blood–brain barrier dysfunction](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/bbb-dysfunction/)** — how immune cells get in.
10. **[Oligodendrocyte biology](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/oligodendrocyte-biology/)** → **[demyelination and remyelination](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/demyelination-remyelination/)** → **[axonal pathology](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/axonal-pathology/)**.
11. **[White matter lesion formation](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/white-matter-lesion-formation/)** — tying pathology to MRI.
12. Bring mechanism to therapy: **[B cell–targeted therapies](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/b-cell-targeted-therapies/)** and **[remyelination therapies](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/remyelination-therapies/)**.
