This pathway traces the biology from immune system fundamentals through the molecular events of lesion formation to the therapeutic targets that modern DMTs exploit.

## Step 1 — Immune system foundations

[Immune System Basics for MS](/mskb/concepts/foundations/immune-system-basics/)

T cells, B cells, MHC class II, HLA-DRB1, and the breakdown of self-tolerance.

## Step 2 — Why MS happens

[Genetic Susceptibility in MS](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/genetic-susceptibility/)

HLA risk loci, GWAS findings, and gene-environment interactions.

[Environmental Risk Factors](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/environmental-risk-factors/)

EBV as a near-necessary cause, vitamin D, smoking, and the molecular mimicry model.

## Step 3 — How lesions form

[Blood-Brain Barrier Dysfunction](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/blood-brain-barrier/)

Tight-junction disruption, lymphocyte transmigration, and gadolinium enhancement.

[Demyelination and Remyelination](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/demyelination-remyelination/)

Oligodendrocyte injury, OPC failure, axonal loss, and the limits of repair.

## Step 4 — From mechanism to biomarker

[MRI Patterns in MS](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/mri-patterns/)

Interpreting lesion types in the context of the underlying pathology.

[CSF and Blood Biomarkers](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/csf-biomarkers/)

OCBs, NfL, and GFAP as windows into ongoing neuroaxonal injury.

## Step 5 — Targeting the mechanism

[B Cell–Targeted Therapies](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/b-cell-therapies/)

Why depleting CD20+ B cells has been the biggest therapeutic advance in MS.

[BTK Inhibitors](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/btk-inhibitors/)

CNS-penetrant agents targeting microglia and compartmentalized inflammation.
