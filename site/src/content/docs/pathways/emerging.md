This pathway is for learners who already understand the clinical and mechanistic basics and want to follow the **research frontier**. It assumes you have worked through the [clinical](/mskb/pathways/clinical/) or [mechanistic](/mskb/pathways/mechanistic/) pathway first.

## Steps

1. **[Progression and PIRA](/mskb/concepts/clinical/progression-pira/)** — progression independent of relapse activity, and why PIRA reframes the relapsing / progressive boundary.
2. **[Smoldering MS](/mskb/concepts/clinical/smoldering-ms/)** — chronic low-grade inflammation and slowly expanding lesions as a unifying frame for progression.
3. **[Paramagnetic rim lesions (PRLs)](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/paramagnetic-rim-lesions/)** — iron-rimmed chronic active lesions as an imaging biomarker of smoldering inflammation.
4. **[Axonal pathology and neurodegeneration](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/axonal-pathology/)** — what is actually driving disability accumulation once inflammation is controlled.
5. **[CSF and blood biomarkers](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/csf-biomarkers/)** → **[prognostic markers](/mskb/concepts/clinical/prognostic-markers/)** — NfL and GFAP as the clinical frontier in fluid biomarkers.
6. **[Optical coherence tomography (OCT)](/mskb/concepts/diagnosis/oct-imaging/)** — retinal thinning as a structural neurodegeneration readout.
7. **[BTK inhibitors](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/btk-inhibitors/)** — BBB-penetrant small molecules targeting B cells *and* microglia, currently the most-watched drug class.
8. **[Remyelination therapies](/mskb/concepts/therapeutics/remyelination-therapies/)** — clemastine, opicinumab, and what "repair" trials have taught us.
9. **[Epstein–Barr virus and MS](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/ebv-and-ms/)** → **[molecular mimicry](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/molecular-mimicry/)** — the EBV-as-cause story and EBNA1 → GlialCAM as the current best-supported mechanism.
10. **[Gut microbiome and MS](/mskb/concepts/mechanisms/gut-microbiome-ms/)** — where the evidence is, and where it is not yet.
