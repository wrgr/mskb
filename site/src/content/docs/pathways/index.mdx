<Aside type="tip" title="Where to start if you're new">
If you're new to MS, begin with the **Clinical** or **Mechanistic** pathway. Once you have the foundations, the **Emerging topics** pathway frames the biggest open problems — the areas where research is moving fastest and where new investigators are most needed.
</Aside>

## How pathways use the concept graph

Each pathway is a curated list of concept references. When you open a pathway, you see the sequence; each step links to its concept page, which in turn links to its prerequisites, papers, and resources. A pathway is the "spine"; concept pages are the "ribs".

## The NMSS Pathways to Cures

The **Emerging topics** pathway is grounded in the National MS Society's [Pathways to Cures Roadmap](https://doi.org/10.1177/13524585241266483) (Bebo et al., 2022 & 2024) — a field-wide strategic agenda developed with input from 300+ people living with MS and endorsed by 30+ MS societies. Its three pillars organize the most important open problems:

| Pillar | Core question |
|--------|--------------|
| **Stop MS** | How do we halt smoldering neuroinflammation, especially in progressive MS? |
| **Restore function** | Can we repair demyelination and protect axons from degeneration? |
| **End MS forever** | Can we prevent MS — beginning with an EBV vaccine? |
