Core MS terms for quick reference while reading concept, topic, and paper pages.

**Autoimmune disease**
: A condition where the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues.

**Axon**
: The long, thin projection of a nerve cell that conducts electrical signals.

**B cell**
: A type of white blood cell that produces antibodies; increasingly recognized as important in MS.

**Blood–brain barrier (BBB)**
: A selective barrier between blood vessels and the brain that normally prevents immune cells from entering the CNS.

**Central nervous system (CNS)**
: The brain and spinal cord.

**Cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)**
: The fluid that surrounds the brain and spinal cord; analyzed to help diagnose MS.

**Clinically isolated syndrome (CIS)**
: A first episode of neurological symptoms suggestive of MS.

**Cytokine**
: A small signaling protein released by cells that affects the behavior of other cells; key player in inflammation.

**Demyelination**
: Loss or damage to myelin, the insulating sheath around nerve fibers.

**Disease-modifying therapy (DMT)**
: A drug that reduces the frequency and severity of MS attacks and slows disability progression.

**EDSS**
: Expanded Disability Status Scale; the most common measure of disability in MS clinical trials.

**Epstein–Barr virus (EBV)**
: A common herpesvirus strongly linked to MS risk; nearly all MS patients have been infected with EBV.

**Experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis (EAE)**
: An animal model of MS where immune attacks on myelin are induced in mice or rats.

**Gadolinium**
: A contrast agent used in MRI to highlight active inflammation (gadolinium-enhancing lesions indicate recent immune activity).

**GWAS**
: Genome-wide association study; a method to find genetic variants associated with disease risk.

**HLA-DRB1**
: A gene in the human leukocyte antigen system; HLA-DRB1\*15:01 is the strongest known genetic MS risk variant.

**Lesion**
: An area of tissue damage; in MS, lesions are areas of demyelination visible on MRI.

**Microglia**
: Immune cells resident in the brain; they can drive inflammation or promote repair.

**Myelin**
: The fatty insulating sheath around nerve fibers that enables fast signal conduction.

**Neurofilament light chain (NfL)**
: A protein released from damaged nerve fibers; used as a blood biomarker of neuronal injury in MS.

**Oligoclonal bands (OCBs)**
: Antibodies found in CSF but not blood, indicating immune activity within the CNS; present in most MS cases.

**Oligodendrocyte**
: The cell type that produces myelin in the CNS.

**Oligodendrocyte precursor cell (OPC)**
: A stem-like cell that can mature into a myelin-producing oligodendrocyte.

**PIRA**
: Progression independent of relapse activity; worsening disability that is not attributable to a relapse.

**Paramagnetic rim lesion (PRL)**
: An MRI-visible chronic active lesion with an iron rim, associated with ongoing smoldering inflammation.

**Primary progressive MS (PPMS)**
: A form of MS with steady worsening from onset, without distinct relapses.

**Relapsing-remitting MS (RRMS)**
: The most common form of MS, with episodic relapses followed by partial or full recovery.

**Remyelination**
: Repair of damaged myelin; a key therapeutic goal in MS research.

**Secondary progressive MS (SPMS)**
: A phase that can follow RRMS and is marked by gradual worsening independent of relapses.

**Smoldering MS**
: Ongoing low-grade inflammation and tissue damage, often associated with PIRA and chronic active lesions.

**T cell**
: A white blood cell type that attacks targets or regulates immune responses; both CD4+ and CD8+ cells are relevant in MS.
