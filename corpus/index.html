Corpus Overview

MSKB is built from a curated corpus of approximately 450–650 peer-reviewed documents spanning 19 research topics in multiple sclerosis. This section documents every significant design decision behind that corpus — not just what was decided, but why.

The corpus specification lives in data/ms_corpus_specification_v1.xlsx and the full audit trail in data/ms_corpus_design_decisions.md.

Three classification systems — different roles

MSKB uses three distinct classification systems. It is important not to conflate them:

SystemSourceRole
T-codes (TOPIC-03–TOPIC-11 + TOPIC-04)Manually defined; assigned via seed propagationDrives corpus selection and balance — the authoritative taxonomy
Leiden citation clusters (9 clusters)Algorithmic; Louvain community detectionAvailable in graph explorer and Citation Topics sidebar; not used in selection
Learner concepts (~30 pages)Pedagogical; authored for site navigationPowers concept pages and T4 signal nomination; not used in selection

See Methodology → Classification systems for full details.

The target range balances coverage depth (~15–35 documents per topic) against review feasibility and RAG retrieval signal-to-noise.

Four-tier architecture

Section titled “Four-tier architecture”

Documents are selected through four distinct mechanisms, each appropriate for a different purpose.

Tier 1 — Seeds

Section titled “Tier 1 — Seeds”

40 hand-curated anchor papers. The baseline remains 2 seeds per topic, with targeted augmentation in thin areas (TOPIC-10 PROs, TOPIC-15 Equity/SDOH, TOPIC-11 Research Priorities). Seeds are conceptual entry points chosen for document-type diversity, citation neighborhood size, and geographic/institutional spread.

See Seeds for the full list with metadata and rationale.

Tier 2 — Cross-seed connected

Section titled “Tier 2 — Cross-seed connected”

Established corpus gate. Tier 2 requires all of:

  • cross_seed_score >= 2
  • kcore >= 4
  • in_degree >= 2
  • paper_importance_score > P70(anchor_category) (category-relative importance threshold)

To avoid systematically under-selecting thin topics, structural gates (kcore, in_degree) are relaxed for topics whose strict-pass share is <2% of the provisional corpus. Cross-seed and category-relative importance requirements remain in force.

Additional signal recorded (but not used as a filter): papers whose connected seeds span ≥ 2 topic codes are flagged as multi-topic bridges — these are often the highest-value papers for cross-topic queries.

Tier 3 — Velocity / emerging

Section titled “Tier 3 — Velocity / emerging”

Topic-capped emerging gate. Tier 3 candidates are papers published 2022+ and accumulating citations in general (citations_per_year >= 20), selected per-topic with:

t3_cap(topic) = max(5, ceil(0.20 * T2_count(topic)))

This ties emerging-paper volume to the size of the established literature in each topic while preserving a floor in sparse areas.

Tier 4 — Expert signal

Section titled “Tier 4 — Expert signal”

Target ~50–60 documents. Selected by explicit expert endorsement only: ECTRIMS/ACTRIMS/AAN best paper awards, NMSS-funded research outputs, Cures Roadmap reference lists, and explicitly non-Western cohort studies. Every T4 document must have its source documented with specific award, year, or rationale. cross_seed_score = 0 is acceptable for T4 if the expert signal is documented.

Expansion sources

Section titled “Expansion sources”

One-hop expansion runs from all 46 sources (40 seeds + 6 review anchors). Cross-seed scoring uses only the 40 seeds; review anchors expand the candidate pool and provide an explicit bridge signal for effective T2 connectivity.

The 6 review anchors (R1–R6) were selected to correct for known structural gaps in the seed citation network:

IDPaperRole
R1Reich et al. 2018 NEJM7 years of forward citation mass not yet in R2 neighborhood
R2Jakimovski et al. 2024 LancetCurrent field-wide overview; primary expansion anchor
R3Kuhlmann et al. 2023 Lancet NeurolGeographic diversity via International Advisory Committee authorship
R4Lancet Neurol 2025 biomarkersBridge for 2022–2025 biomarker acceleration
R5Amezcua et al. 2021 JAMA NeurolDedicated equity expansion (structurally isolated subfield)
R6Montalban et al. 2025 Lancet NeurolGlobal diagnostic criteria with geographically diverse authorship

Why keep R1 alongside R2?

The 2018 Reich et al. NEJM review has accumulated 7 years of forward citations that Jakimovski 2024 has not yet generated. Papers published 2018–2024 will have cited Reich as background. Together they bracket the citation mass of an important period — 2024 captures what’s current; 2018 captures what accumulated in between.

Why a dedicated equity anchor (R5)?

Equity papers are structurally disconnected from the basic science and clinical trial citation network. The cross-seed filter systematically misses equity literature because these papers are not cited by DMT trials or pathophysiology papers — and vice versa. R5 is the principled correction for a known algorithmic limitation, not a workaround.

Connectivity and recency gates

Section titled “Connectivity and recency gates”
  • T2 structural gate: cross_seed_score >= 2, kcore >= 4, in_degree >= 2, and paper_importance_score > P70(anchor_category)
  • T2 low-share relaxation: for topics <2% of strict-pass provisional corpus, drop kcore/in_degree requirements while keeping cross_seed + importance gates
  • T3 gate: year >= 2022 and citations_per_year >= 20, topic-capped at max(5, ceil(0.20 * T2_topic_count))
  • T4: cross_seed_score = 0 acceptable with documented expert signal
  • Final balancing rule: no topic may exceed 20% of the selected corpus (iterative trim from lowest-priority non-seed papers in over-cap topics)

Subdomain score

Section titled “Subdomain score”

subdomain_score = max(citation_percentile, velocity_percentile) computed within each assigned topic, not cross-field.

This remains a ranking and review signal inside topics; it is no longer the hard Tier 3 gate.

Rationale for max() over average: a classic paper with high historical citations but low velocity is still valuable (foundational but superseded); a new paper with high velocity but modest total citations is also valuable (emerging and important). The max captures both without requiring both simultaneously.

Rationale for within-subdomain normalization: citation volume varies systematically between subfields. Equity papers generate far fewer citations than landmark trials — not because they matter less but because citation practices differ. Within-subdomain percentiles correct for this: equity papers compete against equity papers.

QA overlap check

Section titled “QA overlap check”

After pipeline completion, for each review anchor R1–R6 compute:

  • > 70% — seeds well-chosen ✓
  • 60–70% — acceptable, monitor
  • < 60% for a specific topic — seeds thin → flag in Gap Tracker, augment seeds, or add T4 documents

Topic organization

Section titled “Topic organization”

19 topics (18 + TOPIC-04 Natural History) organized in three layers:

  • Foundation (TOPIC-03–TOPIC-05) — biological vocabulary and disease framing
  • Clinical (TOPIC-06–TOPIC-13) — diagnosis, treatment, management
  • Context/Future (TOPIC-14–TOPIC-11) — equity, pediatrics, AI, frontiers

The layering reflects epistemological dependencies, not value judgments. Equity research (TOPIC-15) is in the Context/Future layer because its critical function — interrogating assumptions embedded in the clinical literature — is most meaningful after the Clinical baseline has been established.

See Topic Map for all 19 topics with seed assignments and document targets.

Known gaps

Section titled “Known gaps”

12 tracked gaps are documented in the Gap Tracker, covering Global South representation, Hispanic/Asian MS cohort coverage, qualitative and lived-experience research, digital biomarkers, non-English literature, and pipeline mechanics. Gaps are tracked publicly rather than papered over.
