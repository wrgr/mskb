Topic Map

The MSKB corpus covers 18 research topics (TOPIC-00 through TOPIC-17) organized in three reading layers. The baseline is 2 T1 seeds per topic, with targeted seed augmentation where citation-structure coverage is thin or mechanistically distinct sub-areas need dedicated anchors.

Read order: Foundation → Clinical → Context/Future. You can enter at any layer but the epistemological dependencies flow in this direction.

TOPIC-## codes vs tier codes (T1–T4)

MSKB uses two separate code systems that previously shared the “T” prefix — now disambiguated:

  • TOPIC-## codes (this page) — the primary corpus taxonomy used for selection and balance. TOPIC-00 through TOPIC-17.
  • Tier codes T1–T4 — corpus tiers by selection mechanism (T1 seeds, T2 cross-seed connected, T3 velocity/emerging, T4 expert signal).

The other classification system — Leiden citation clusters (9 clusters, bibliometric) — is available in the Citation Topics sidebar and explorer_graph.json as an alternative view; not used in selection or balance.

See Methodology for a full explanation.

Foundation layer

Section titled “Foundation layer”

Topics that establish the biological vocabulary and disease framing prerequisite to all subsequent topics.

TOPIC-00 — Disease Overview & Phenotypes Foundation

Section titled “TOPIC-00 — Disease Overview & Phenotypes ”

Why it matters: RRMS/SPMS/PPMS/CIS/RIS taxonomy; diagnostic criteria; prerequisite vocabulary for all other topics.

Every other topic in the corpus presupposes the phenotype vocabulary and diagnostic criteria established here. The 2017 McDonald criteria (seed MS-T1-002) are the operational definition of MS used in all clinical trials and clinical care.

SeedsMS-T1-001 (Compston & Coles 2008, Lancet) · MS-T1-002 (Thompson et al. 2018 McDonald criteria, Lancet Neurol)
Target25–30 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review · Consensus/Guideline

TOPIC-01 — Immunology & Genetics Foundation

Section titled “TOPIC-01 — Immunology & Genetics ”

Why it matters: HLA architecture; 200+ GWAS loci; polygenic risk; why MS is an immune-dysregulation disease.

The IMSGC GWAS (2019) identified 233 risk variants and established that MS is genetically an immune-dysregulation disease — the majority of non-HLA loci are expressed in immune cells. This underpins the rationale for immunotherapy as the primary therapeutic strategy.

SeedsMS-T1-007 (IMSGC GWAS 2019, Science) · MS-T1-008 (Patsopoulos 2018, CSHP Med)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesEpidemiological/Burden · Mechanistic Review

TOPIC-02 — Pathophysiology Foundation

Section titled “TOPIC-02 — Pathophysiology ”

Why it matters: Demyelination vs neurodegeneration duality; lesion biology; why peripheral immune drugs fail in progressive MS.

The immune-vs-neurodegeneration framing (Trapp & Nave 2008) explains why treatments that suppress peripheral inflammation can control relapses but fail to prevent disability progression. Understanding lesion heterogeneity (Lassmann 2018) explains why imaging correlates imperfectly with disability.

SeedsMS-T1-005 (Lassmann 2018, CSHP Med) · MS-T1-006 (Trapp & Nave 2008, Ann Rev Neurosci)
Target25–30 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review

TOPIC-03 — Epidemiology & Global Burden Foundation

Section titled “TOPIC-03 — Epidemiology & Global Burden ”

Why it matters: Quantitative global frame; geographic diversity; disease scale worldwide.

The global burden framing establishes scale (2.8 million people with MS worldwide), geographic distribution, incidence and prevalence trends, and mortality patterns. It is the context within which all clinical and mechanistic topics operate and provides the baseline against which intervention effects are measured at population level.

SeedsMS-T1-035 (Atlas of MS 3rd ed, Walton/MSIF 2020) · MS-T1-036 (GBD 2016, Lancet Neurol 2019)
Target25–30 documents
Primary doc typesEpidemiological/Burden · Systematic Review/Meta-Analysis

TOPIC-04 — Natural History Foundation

Section titled “TOPIC-04 — Natural History ”

Why it matters: Pre-DMT disease trajectory; disability progression independent of relapses; baseline for treatment effect measurement.

The Lyon cohort (Confavreux et al. 2000, NEJM) established that disability progression, once underway, proceeds independently of prior or concurrent relapse activity. This finding is the conceptual foundation for the entire progressive MS and smoldering disease research agenda — it directly challenged the assumption that suppressing relapses would prevent long-term disability.

Post-rerun monitoring: verify that TOPIC-04 generates a distinct corpus from TOPIC-09 — the Lyon cohort citation neighborhood overlaps substantially with progressive MS literature.

SeedsMS-T1-003 (Confavreux et al. 2000 Lyon cohort, NEJM) · MS-T1-004 (Confavreux & Vukusic 2006, Brain)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesNatural History/Registry

TOPIC-05 — Risk Factors & Etiology Foundation

Section titled “TOPIC-05 — Risk Factors & Etiology ”

Why it matters: EBV; vitamin D; smoking; latitude; multi-factor model; emerging vs settled science.

The Bjornevik 2022 Science paper (10 million US military serum samples) established a near-causal relationship between EBV infection and subsequent MS. This is the most significant etiological finding in recent MS research and reshapes how the field thinks about prevention.

SeedsMS-T1-009 (Bjornevik et al. 2022 EBV, Science) · MS-T1-010 (Ascherio & Munger 2007, Ann Neurol)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesEpidemiological/Burden · Mechanistic Review

Clinical layer

Section titled “Clinical layer”

Topics presupposing the Foundation vocabulary — diagnosis, treatment, and management.

TOPIC-06 — Diagnosis & Monitoring Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-06 — Diagnosis & Monitoring ”

Why it matters: MRI criteria; gadolinium; PRL; central vein sign; evolving McDonald criteria.

MRI is the primary diagnostic and monitoring tool in MS. The MAGNIMS-CMSC-NAIMS consensus (2021) governs MRI monitoring in clinical practice. The Absinta 2019 paramagnetic rim lesion (PRL) paper connects imaging to the smoldering disease mechanism and bridges TOPIC-06 and TOPIC-09.

SeedsMS-T1-011 (Wattjes et al. 2021 MAGNIMS consensus, Lancet Neurol) · MS-T1-012 (Absinta et al. 2019 PRL, JAMA Neurol)
Target25–30 documents
Primary doc typesConsensus/Guideline · Epidemiological/Burden

TOPIC-07 — Biomarkers Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-07 — Biomarkers ”

Why it matters: NfL; GFAP; CHI3L1; serum vs CSF; treatment monitoring; prognostic use; 2024 biomarker-integrated McDonald criteria.

Neurofilament light chain (NfL) is the first blood biomarker to enter routine MS clinical practice for treatment monitoring. The Khalil 2018 Nat Rev Neurology review anchors the NfL literature; v1.3 adds GFAP (Abdelhak 2022) to address the broader glial damage biomarker class and the 2024 McDonald criteria update (Montalban 2025) as the authoritative integration of serum biomarkers into diagnosis. The prior second NfL seed (Bittner 2021 Brain) was retired — it extensively overlapped with Khalil 2018 and biased expansion toward NfL at the expense of GFAP and CHI3L1. The framing anchor R4 (Lancet Neurology 2025 biomarker review) provides additional coverage for CHI3L1 and multi-analyte panels.

SeedsMS-T1-013 (Khalil et al. 2018 NfL, Nat Rev Neurol) · MS-T1-044 (Abdelhak et al. 2022 GFAP, Nat Rev Neurol) · MS-T1-045 (Montalban et al. 2025 McDonald criteria 2024, Lancet Neurol)
Target25–30 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review · Consensus/Guideline
Retired seedMS-T1-014 (Bittner 2021 serum NfL, Brain) — retired v1.3; citation neighborhood overlapped >80% with MS-T1-013

TOPIC-08 — Disease-Modifying Therapies Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-08 — Disease-Modifying Therapies ”

Why it matters: DMT classes; escalation vs treat-to-target; mechanism diversity across anti-CD20, VLA-4, and S1P; BTK inhibitors emerging.

v1.3 rebalanced: ORATORIO (MS-T1-016, PPMS trial) redistributed to TOPIC-09 where it belongs mechanistically. Seeds now span three drug mechanisms: OPERA (ocrelizumab, anti-CD20 B-cell depletion, RRMS), AFFIRM (natalizumab, VLA-4 integrin, RRMS), and EXPAND (siponimod, S1P modulation, SPMS). This ensures the T2 expansion reaches the natalizumab, S1P modulator, and SPMS treatment literature, not just the B-cell depletion cluster. BTK inhibitors (tolebrutinib, fenebrutinib — Phase 3 ongoing) and alemtuzumab are not yet seeded; monitor via T3 velocity filter.

SeedsMS-T1-015 (Hauser et al. 2017 OPERA, NEJM) · MS-T1-042 (Polman et al. 2006 AFFIRM, NEJM) · MS-T1-043 (Kappos et al. 2018 EXPAND, Lancet)
Target30–35 documents
Primary doc typesLandmark RCT

TOPIC-09 — Progressive MS & Smoldering Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-09 — Progressive MS & Smoldering ”

Why it matters: PIRA; smoldering inflammation; why relapse suppression ≠ disability prevention; biggest unmet need.

Progression independent of relapse activity (PIRA) is the primary driver of long-term disability in MS. The smoldering MS framework (Giovannoni et al. 2022) argues that chronic, low-grade CNS inflammation persists even when peripheral inflammation is suppressed — explaining why highly effective DMTs fail to fully prevent disability accumulation. Ontaneda et al. 2016 (Lancet) provides the research-agenda bridge across progressive disease biology, trial strategy, and repair. ORATORIO (Montalban 2017 NEJM) redistributed here from TOPIC-08 v1.3 — as the pivotal PPMS trial its citation neighborhood is progressive-MS-specific, not RRMS-specific.

SeedsMS-T1-017 (Lublin et al. 2014 phenotype definitions, Neurology) · MS-T1-018 (Giovannoni et al. 2022 smoldering MS, TAND) · MS-T1-040 (Ontaneda et al. 2016 progressive MS prospects, Lancet) · MS-T1-016 (Montalban et al. 2017 ORATORIO PPMS, NEJM)
Target25–30 documents
Primary doc typesConsensus/Guideline · Mechanistic Review · Landmark RCT

TOPIC-10 — Patient-Reported Outcomes Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-10 — Patient-Reported Outcomes ”

Why it matters: EDSS limitations; fatigue; cognition; PRO instruments; trial endpoint gaps; digital health.

EDSS is the primary disability scale in MS but is insensitive to fatigue, cognition, and upper extremity function — domains that matter most to patients. This topic covers the PRO instrument landscape and the growing literature on composite endpoints and digital monitoring.

SeedsMS-T1-019 (Ziemssen et al. 2019 PROs, MSRD) · MS-T1-020 (Ontaneda et al. 2019 MSOAC, Neurology) · MS-T1-037 (Benedict et al. 2017 SDMT validation, MSJ) · MS-T1-038 (Hobart et al. 2001 MSIS-29, Brain)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review · Epidemiological/Burden

TOPIC-11 — Symptom Management & Multidisciplinary Care Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-11 — Symptom Management & Multidisciplinary Care ”

Why it matters: Exercise as therapy; multidisciplinary rehabilitation; fatigue management; bladder/bowel; non-pharmacological care; implementation into MS programs.

Symptom management and multidisciplinary rehabilitation address the aspects of MS burden most important to patients — fatigue, mobility, bladder/bowel function, cognitive difficulties — that are incompletely addressed by DMTs alone. This literature was previously distributed across TOPIC-10 (PROs) and TOPIC-12 (comorbidities); TOPIC-11 creates a dedicated home for the intervention evidence. The Dalgas 2019 JAMA Neurol editorial frames exercise as disease-modifying; Khan & Amatya 2017 is the most comprehensive umbrella review of multidisciplinary rehabilitation evidence. T4 entries T4-035 through T4-041 (rehabilitation/exercise expert signal) naturally anchor the T2 expansion here.

SeedsMS-T1-047 (Dalgas et al. 2019 exercise as medicine, JAMA Neurol) · MS-T1-048 (Khan & Amatya 2017 rehabilitation systematic review, Arch Phys Med Rehabil)
Target15–20 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review · Systematic Review/Meta-Analysis

TOPIC-12 — Comorbidities Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-12 — Comorbidities ”

Why it matters: Depression; anxiety; cognitive impairment; bladder dysfunction; interaction with disability.

Psychiatric comorbidity affects ~50% of people with MS and amplifies disability, reduces quality of life, and complicates treatment decisions. The Marrie 2015 systematic review established the epidemiological baseline for comorbidity burden; Chiaravalloti & DeLuca 2008 remains the foundational cognitive neuroscience reference.

SeedsMS-T1-021 (Marrie et al. 2015 psychiatric comorbidity, MSJ) · MS-T1-022 (Chiaravalloti & DeLuca 2008 cognition, Lancet Neurol)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesSystematic Review/Meta-Analysis · Mechanistic Review

TOPIC-13 — Pregnancy & MS Clinical

Section titled “TOPIC-13 — Pregnancy & MS ”

Why it matters: Relapse-rebound risk; DMT teratogenicity; washout; post-partum; breastfeeding.

The PRIMS cohort (Confavreux et al. 1998, NEJM) established the pregnancy relapse trajectory — protective during the third trimester, rebound risk post-partum — cited by every subsequent pregnancy/MS paper. The Krysko 2023 consensus provides the current clinical guidance on DMT use in pregnancy.

SeedsMS-T1-023 (Confavreux et al. 1998 PRIMS, NEJM) · MS-T1-024 (Krysko et al. 2023 family planning, Lancet Neurol)
Target15–20 documents
Primary doc typesNatural History/Registry · Consensus/Guideline

Context/Future layer

Section titled “Context/Future layer”

Topics most meaningful after establishing the Clinical baseline.

TOPIC-14 — Pediatric MS Context/Future

Section titled “TOPIC-14 — Pediatric MS ”

Why it matters: ADEM differential; neurodevelopmental stakes; IPMSSG criteria; pediatric EBV data.

MS in children and adolescents has distinct diagnostic challenges (differential from ADEM and other demyelinating diseases), higher relapse rates, and neurodevelopmental stakes not present in adult disease. The PARADIGMS trial (Chitnis et al. 2018, NEJM) is the only head-to-head DMT RCT in a pediatric MS population.

SeedsMS-T1-025 (Krupp et al. 2013 IPMSSG criteria, MSJ) · MS-T1-026 (Chitnis et al. 2018 PARADIGMS, NEJM)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesConsensus/Guideline · Landmark RCT

TOPIC-15 — Equity & SDOH Context/Future

Section titled “TOPIC-15 — Equity & SDOH ”

Why it matters: Structural racism; Black/Hispanic disparities; diagnostic delay; access to high-efficacy DMTs.

Equity research is placed in the Context/Future layer as a reading recommendation, not a value judgment. Its critical function — interrogating assumptions embedded in the clinical literature — is most powerful when read against the Clinical baseline it critiques.

TOPIC-15 uses five complementary seeds spanning prevalence (Wallin 2023, Langer-Gould 2022), incidence (Langer-Gould 2013), SES-severity outcomes (Gray-Roncal 2021), and DMT access/response disparities for African American patients (Okai 2022 — promoted from T4-017 expert signal in v1.3). The dedicated review anchor R5 (Amezcua et al. 2021) remains the bridge for structurally disconnected equity literature.

SeedsMS-T1-027 (Wallin et al. 2023 prevalence by race/ethnicity, JAMA Neurol) · MS-T1-028 (Langer-Gould et al. 2022 disparities prevalence, Neurology) · MS-T1-039 (Langer-Gould et al. 2013 incidence across groups, Neurology) · MS-T1-041 (Gray-Roncal et al. 2021 SES-severity, Neurology) · MS-T1-046 (Okai et al. 2022 African American DMT outcomes, Neurology)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesEquity/SDOH

TOPIC-16 — Clinical AI & Learning Systems Context/Future

Section titled “TOPIC-16 — Clinical AI & Learning Systems ”

Why it matters: Lesion segmentation; progression prediction; treatment recommendation; data-driven disease reclassification; implementation gap; explainability.

Clinical AI in MS spans lesion segmentation (mature, deployed), progression prediction (active research), treatment recommendation (early stage), and data-driven phenotype reclassification (emerging). Louapre 2025 Nature Medicine (MS-T1-031) added v1.3 — redistributed from TOPIC-17, where it was structurally incompatible with the remyelination biology seeds. AI-based reclassification uses longitudinal clinical data to challenge the traditional RRMS/SPMS/PPMS taxonomy and belongs in the Clinical AI topic.

SeedsMS-T1-029 (Prados et al. 2024 AI review, Curr Neurol Neurosci Rep) · MS-T1-030 (Noteboom et al. 2025 big data, J NeuroEng Rehab) · MS-T1-031 (Louapre et al. 2025 AI reclassification, Nat Med)
Target20–25 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review · Epidemiological/Burden

TOPIC-17 — Remyelination & Neuroprotection Context/Future

Section titled “TOPIC-17 — Remyelination & Neuroprotection ”

Why it matters: Remyelination biology; neuroprotection strategies; repair beyond inflammation suppression; EBV vaccine prevention; biggest scientific frontier.

v1.3 renamed from “Emerging Frontiers” for clean, coherent scope. AI reclassification (Louapre 2025) redistributed to TOPIC-16 — it belongs with Clinical AI, not with repair biology. Seeds now both anchor the remyelination-to-clinic pipeline: Franklin & Ffrench-Constant 2008 (Nat Rev Neurosci) is the foundational remyelination biology review; Lubetzki et al. 2020 (Lancet Neurol) covers the basic-science-to-clinical-translation pipeline for remyelination therapies specifically. EBV vaccine prevention (G-005) remains a future gap — no Phase 2/3 efficacy data yet.

SeedsMS-T1-032 (Franklin & Ffrench-Constant 2008 remyelination, Nat Rev Neurosci) · MS-T1-049 (Lubetzki et al. 2020 remyelination to clinic, Lancet Neurol)
Target15–20 documents
Primary doc typesMechanistic Review

Research Priorities topic retired in v1.3

Research Priorities & Cures Roadmap (formerly TOPIC-16 (pre-v1.3 numbering)) was retired in v1.3. The Roadmap papers (MS-T1-033 Bebo 2022 and MS-T1-034 Bebo 2024) are now framing seeds R7 and R8, unlocking reference-list expansion algorithmically across all topics rather than generating a narrow standalone cluster.
