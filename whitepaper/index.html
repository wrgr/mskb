Whitepaper

MSKB: A Bibliometric Knowledge Engineering Framework for MS Research Navigation

Section titled “MSKB: A Bibliometric Knowledge Engineering Framework for MS Research Navigation”

Version 1.0 · April 2026

Download the PDF

A print-ready version of this whitepaper is available for download.

Download WHITEPAPER.pdf →

Abstract

Section titled “Abstract”

The Multiple Sclerosis Knowledge Base (MSKB) is an open-source knowledge engineering framework that transforms the MS research literature into a structured, pedagogically scaffolded knowledge base navigable by undergraduate researchers. MSKB combines bibliometric graph analysis, multi-signal relevance scoring, expert-curated ontology design, and large language model summarization to select, organize, and present approximately 500–650 peer-reviewed MS papers.

Unlike prior literature aggregation tools, MSKB:

  1. Makes corpus construction decisions explicit and auditable
  2. Embeds pedagogical scaffolding through a learner-centered concept ontology grounded in Bloom’s taxonomy
  3. Corrects for known structural biases in citation networks that systematically exclude equity and emerging-subfield literature
  4. Exposes a heterogeneous knowledge graph connecting papers, topics, biomedical entities, and learner journey paths

Motivation

Section titled “Motivation”

Multiple sclerosis research spans basic immunology and genetics, clinical trial methodology, neuroimaging, neurorehabilitation, epidemiology, health equity, and patient-reported outcomes. A student entering the field faces a body of literature that is simultaneously vast, structurally heterogeneous, and rapidly evolving.

Existing tools — PubMed, Google Scholar, OpenAlex — are retrieval systems, not learning systems. They answer specific queries but do not help a learner understand what they need to know before they can ask the right queries, which papers are foundational versus confirmatory, or how the research landscape has evolved over time.

MSKB addresses this gap through a multi-stage pipeline that outputs a curated, navigable knowledge base designed specifically for undergraduate researchers.

Design Principles

Section titled “Design Principles”

Three principles govern all MSKB design decisions:

Transparency over automation. Every significant corpus construction decision is documented in a human-readable audit trail alongside the code that implements it. Algorithmic methods are used where they add value, but their limitations are explicitly flagged.

Structural over prestige. Corpus selection relies on citation network structure (cross-seed connectivity, PageRank, k-core) rather than journal impact factors or institutional prestige.

Pedagogy as a first-class concern. Every design decision is evaluated against whether it helps an undergraduate researcher build genuine understanding, not merely access information.

Pipeline Overview

Section titled “Pipeline Overview”

MSKB is an 18-stage reproducible pipeline that transforms raw OpenAlex data into a static, client-rendered knowledge base site:

StageDescription
0Seed governance
1Corpus retrieval (OpenAlex)
2Deduplication and version merging
3Graph construction (citation, co-citation, bibliographic coupling, co-authorship)
4Multi-signal relevance scoring
5Topic discovery (Leiden community detection)
5bTopic evidence assignment
5cCore corpus selection (T1–T4 tier rules)
6Abstract backfill (PubMed)
7Learner journey construction
8Paper distillation (Claude API)
9Concept–paper linking (Gemini / Anthropic)
10Kid journey and topic overview generation
11Knowledge graph construction
12Corpus audit (automated quality gates)
13–18Metrics export, site generation, corpus export

Four-Tier Corpus Architecture

Section titled “Four-Tier Corpus Architecture”

Papers are assigned to one of four tiers based on how they enter the corpus:

TierNameSelection Mechanism
T1SeedsHuman curation: topic coverage, document type diversity
T2Cross-seed connectedAlgorithmic: structural centrality in the seed citation network
T3Velocity / EmergingAlgorithmic: high velocity relative to within-topic peers
T4Expert signalHuman: conference awards, non-Western cohort studies, structurally isolated important papers

Novel Contributions

Section titled “Novel Contributions”
Auditable corpus construction Every significant design decision documented as a first-class engineering artifact with explicit rationale and engineering-judgment callouts.
Structural equity correction Four explicit mechanisms to correct for citation network biases that systematically disadvantage equity and emerging-subfield literature.
Pedagogical ontology bound to corpus The MS-LCO binds Bloom's-taxonomy-grounded concept structure to the curated corpus through an automated, refreshable concept–paper linking pipeline.
Heterogeneous learner knowledge graph A single graph unifying bibliometric relationships, biomedical entity mentions, and pedagogical learner journey edges.

Known Limitations

Section titled “Known Limitations”
  • OpenAlex coverage gaps — some conference proceedings and gray literature are not indexed
  • Regex entity extraction — entity tagging uses pattern matching, not neural NER; false positives and negatives exist
  • LLM faithfulness — paper distillations are spot-checked but not systematically evaluated; treat as reading aids, not substitutes
  • Static corpus update cycle — the corpus is a snapshot; major new results are not automatically incorporated
  • Threshold calibration — numeric inclusion thresholds were calibrated by inspection, not against retrieval quality benchmarks
